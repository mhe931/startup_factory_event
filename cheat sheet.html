Framework: C.R.E.A.T.E Strategy: Chain-of-Thought

C The C.R.E.A.T.E Framework

  • Context: Give the background. What is the startup stage? What is the goal?
  • Role: Assign a persona. PM, Senior Dev, or Skeptical Investor.
  • Explicit Instructions: List exactly what to do. Use verbs.
  • Audience: Who is reading the result? Students? VCs? Mentors?
  • Tone: Define the voice. Visionary, professional, or grounded.
  • Evaluation: Set the format. Table, JSON, or 5 bullet points.

Essential Power Triggers

"Think step-by-step."

Forces the model to reason and reduces logic errors in math or strategy.

"Explain your reasoning first."

Useful for finding flaws in your own business assumptions.

"Cite your sources or say you don't know."

Prevents the AI from making up fake market statistics.

The Stress-Test Persona: Skeptical VC

"Act as a Senior Partner at a Tier-1 VC. I will present my startup idea. Your goal is to find 3 fatal flaws in my unit economics and market defensibility. Do not be polite. Be hyper-rational and data-driven."

Why use this? It simulates a real pitch scenario. If your idea survives this prompt, it's ready for a real mentor session at Startup Factory.

Truth & Verification

  • ✓ Ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you in this number?"
  • ✓ Command: "Check your own output for contradictions."
  • ✓ Input data: Provide your own market research as a context block.
