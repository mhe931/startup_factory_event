The Entrepreneurial Shift

AI is your first hire, not your search engine.

Users Ask

They type simple queries and expect instant perfection. This is the Google mindset.

Engineers Design

They build structured prompts, define roles, and guide logic. This is the Systems mindset.

Logic & Chain-of-Thought

"Think step-by-step."

  • AI models don't "think"—they probabilistically predict.
  • Complex logic requested instantly often leads to hallucination.
  • Forcing the model to show its math reduces error rates significantly.
14:10 – 15:30 | Logic & The Skeptical VC
Goal: Stress-test the business model using Chain-of-Thought (CoT).
Däniel's Script:
"Welcome back. Now we get serious. AI can hallucinate. It makes things up. To fix this, we use Chain-of-Thought. We tell it: 'Think step-by-step.' This forces the model to show its math before giving an answer.
The "Skeptical VC" Exercise (30 mins):
We will use the "Skeptical VC" persona to challenge your business idea. The AI will act as a venture capitalist who has seen thousands of pitches and is looking for holes in your logic.
Step-by-Step Exercise:
Take your business idea from the previous exercise.
Write a prompt asking the AI to act as a Skeptical VC and analyze your idea.
Use Chain-of-Thought by asking: "Think step-by-step and show your math."
Share the AI's response with your neighbor. Did it find any holes?
Däniel's Pro-Tip:
"If the AI immediately says 'Great idea!', it's not being skeptical enough. You need to push it to find the risks."

The Skeptical VC Persona

Stress-testing your business model before a real pitch. 


# SYSTEM PROMPT
Act as a Senior Partner at a VC firm.
Personality: Highly analytical, ruthlessly pragmatic. Don't be nice.

# USER PROMPT

Task:
1. Find 3 fatal flaws in my logic.
2. Ask me 3 questions that would make a founder sweat.
We are also going to use the 'Skeptical VC' persona. This is a prompt that tells the AI to be your harshest critic. It's better to fail here than in a real pitch."
